## ⚖️ Ethical Issues / AI and Societal Changes

### June 2025 — Your Brain on ChatGPT: Accumulation of Cognitive Debt when Using an AI Assistant for Essay Writing Task
**Paper:** [Your Brain on ChatGPT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.08872)  

**Summary:**  
This MIT study explores how reliance on AI tools like ChatGPT can lead to the **accumulation of “cognitive debt”** — a subtle reduction in users’ independent reasoning and long-term skill development.  

While the headline takeaway is that “AI makes us worse,” the paper emphasizes a **critical nuance**: the negative effects arise primarily when AI is **used incorrectly**, such as bypassing essential stages of critical thinking or over-relying on AI outputs without reflection.  

The findings underline the importance of **structured interaction protocols** and **active cognitive engagement** when using AI for intellectual tasks like essay writing.

### 2025 — The Impact of Generative AI on Critical Thinking: Self-Reported Reductions in Cognitive Effort and Confidence Effects From a Survey of Knowledge Workers
**Paper:** [Microsoft Study (MSR Link)](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/lee_2025_ai_critical_thinking_survey.pdf)  

**Summary:**  
This Microsoft research examines the self-reported impacts of generative AI on critical thinking across a broad set of knowledge workers.  

The survey data reveal **consistent reductions in perceived cognitive effort** and **shifts in confidence levels** when individuals use AI tools to assist with knowledge work.  

While users feel more efficient, the study warns that this may mask a **decline in deep analytical engagement**, potentially affecting long-term expertise development.  

The authors call for **design interventions and educational strategies** to help workers maintain critical thinking skills in AI-augmented workflows.
