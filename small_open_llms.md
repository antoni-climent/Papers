</details> <details> <summary><strong>May 2025 — Grokking in the Wild</strong></summary>

Paper: arXiv

Title: Grokking in the Wild: Data Augmentation for Real-World Multi-Hop Reasoning with Transformers
Summary: Demonstrates delayed generalization effects (grokking) in real-world multi-hop QA tasks using GPT-2 small. Remarkably, this approach outperforms larger LLMs such as GPT-4o and O1-mini on the same reasoning benchmarks, highlighting how data augmentation can unlock latent reasoning capabilities in small models.
Blog post

</details> <details> <summary><strong>July 2025 — Mixture-of-Recursions</strong></summary>

Paper: arXiv

Summary: A new architecture proposed by Google DeepMind that allows models to dynamically learn recursive depths per token, enabling adaptive computation. This makes Transformer-based language models more efficient and compact, opening pathways for flexible reasoning at lower compute cost.
